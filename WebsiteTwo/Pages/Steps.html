Caledonia Steps

Steps:

  • I imported a picture of my grandparents’ home in Morganton, North Carolina as the background.


  • For the second layer, I imported a selfie over top of the background picture.


  • Then I used the eraser tool to “delete” everything that wasn’t just my face. The eraser tool doesn’t delete anything, rather it blends the two pictures together. It covers the background picture overtop the first, wherever you use the tool.


    • There is another way to do step 2 and 3:

    You can import the second layer into another Ps tab.

    Use the pen tool to painstakingly trace around the face.

    Then drag and drop the image onto the background layer.

    Lastly, you may still need to use the eraser tool.

  • Move the image to the upper right side.


  • Click effects/blend mode (can be found either in search or on the side of your screen above your layers, ‘Normal Button’) to change opacity and color. Shortcuts: Hold down shift and + to quickly scroll up and shift and - to quickly scroll down .


  • Use eraser tool again to blend more, if needed.

    • Blur

  • Click the filter menu > Blur > Field Blur


  • Place 1 circle in the upper left and lower right screen and adjust the blur effect.

    • Text

  • This can be found on the left side of your screen. Simply click on the button, and move the empty rectangle where you want the text to be.


  • Just like in a word document, you can change the color, font, and size of text.


  • I wrote Caledonia in a creme-brown color, papyrus font and approx 48 font.

    • Inspiration for Caledonia



    The inspiration for the project came to me while listening to the scottish folk song by Dougie MacLean. Caledonia is the ancient name for Scotland, but more than that it represents home. I’m not scottish nor is anyone in my family, but I could relate to the meaning of the song, missing home or familiarity. It was then that I knew what I wanted for the background and central theme of the project. I choose the mountains behind my grandparents home, because it reminds me of them and the summers of my youth.

    To Wrap Up,

    Looking back at this project, it should take me no time to duplicate this again, but when I first created this, it took me a good 4 hours to complete. I have to say that by the end, it was frustrating figuring out how to export the file or picture. After spending hours on a project, it’s important to know how to save it, so here’s a guide to do just that:

    To export image:

    Click SAVE AS > name it.jpg > click format > scroll down to jpg click enter.

    To export file:

    SAVE AS > Format> PSD> name file
